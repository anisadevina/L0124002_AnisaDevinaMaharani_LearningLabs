Iconic Fashion Trends

Lolita

A fashion style inspired by Victorian clothing and Rococo influences, featuring dresses with lace, bows, and frills. It emphasizes modesty with a youthful, doll-like appearance. The trend often includes accessories like bonnets and knee-high socks.

Y2K

Short for "Year 2000," this trend draws inspiration from the late 1990s and early 2000s, characterized by flashy, futuristic styles, metallic fabrics, and low-rise jeans. Think of things like butterfly clips, cargo pants, and denim everything.

Vintage

Fashion that imitates styles from the past, typically from the 1920s to the 1980s. It includes classic pieces like retro dresses, high-waisted pants, and tailored coats. Vintage is all about embracing the timeless charm of older clothing.

Bohemian

Often associated with a free-spirited, artistic lifestyle, this style includes flowing, relaxed clothing with earthy colors, fringe, floral patterns, and ethnic-inspired accessories. It mixes layers and textures for an effortlessly cool look.

Coquette

A playful and flirtatious fashion style that includes feminine and soft clothing. Key elements include lace, ruffles, pastel colors, and lots of bows. It has a slightly provocative yet charming vibe, often resembling a modern take on Victorian-era elegance.

Fairycore

A whimsical and ethereal trend, inspired by fantasy and nature, often featuring soft, pastel-colored clothing, flowy fabrics, and floral accents. The vibe is light and dreamy, like stepping into a fairy tale world.

Cottagecore

A romanticized rural aesthetic that embraces country life. It includes items like flowy dresses, floral prints, and vintage-inspired pieces. The look reflects a cozy, simple lifestyle surrounded by nature, often with elements like straw hats and gingham.

Kawaii

A Japanese fashion trend that translates to "cute." It focuses on youthful, playful styles with bright colors, oversized bows, cartoon characters, and accessories like plush toys or animal ears. The overall vibe is fun, innocent, and cheerful.

Goth

A darker, more alternative fashion style, characterized by black clothing, heavy makeup (especially dark eyeliner), and edgy accessories like studs or chokers. It has roots in punk and Victorian fashion, often with a moody, dramatic flair.
